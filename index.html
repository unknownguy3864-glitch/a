For You

This is not a request.
Not a question.
Just a memory — written with love.

Chapter 1 — How We Met

On 23rd November 2023, two strangers met on Tinder. No expectations. No plans. Just words.

We talked for two days — nothing dramatic, you used to copy my texts. When I asked you why, you smiled and said: “Is that a crime?” And honestly… that was one of the cutest things I had ever heard. just conversations that felt calm and familiar.

On 26th November, I confessed my feelings. You didn’t say yes or no. You asked for time — because your exams mattered.

I waited.

And on 2nd December, you chose me. and our love story begin.

Chapter 2 — The Day I Fell

On 7th December, we fought. A real fight. Words hurt. Silence hurt more. But when things finally calmed down, I found out something that changed me forever. You had cried… because of me. For me. And in that moment, I made a silent promise to myself — I will never be the reason for her tears again. That day, I didn’t just love you. I chose you..

Chapter 3 — You Changed Me

You weren’t just my girlfriend. You were the person who changed me.

You fought for us. You cared deeply. You got jealous — and somehow made it look adorable.

Every time you said “breakup” and came back saying “I can’t live without you,”

I fell harder. We were long distance. We never met. Never held hands.

But we planned everything.

“What if you come here?” “What will we do?” “How will it feel?”

Even without touching, we built a world. For one full year, every morning started with your picture and a beautiful message. And even though we fought — often at the end of every month — we always came back. Because love kept choosing us.

Chapter 4 — A Love Without Touch

We were long distance.

We never met. Never held hands. Never hugged.

Yet we saw each other on video calls, planned futures, imagined moments.

Destiny never let us meet, even though we desperately wanted to.

Still, for one full year, we loved each other deeply.

Chapter 5 — When Distance Grew

I didn’t know how to love calmly — I only knew how to love deeply. Still, we stayed. We fought the situation. Not each other. .

Chapter 6 — Busy Days, Quiet Nights

You joined school. Life became busy.

From endless conversations, we came down to one hour a day.

I tried to understand. I tried to adjust. but i stand for you

Chapter 7 — Birthdays & Hope

July came with silence. One full month of fear and waiting.

I was scared.Broken. But then came your birthday — 29th July. I reached out. I tried to make you smile. To remind you how special you were.

We talked again.

Maybe that was us — breaking and choosing each other again and again.

The Questions I Still Carry

I don’t really know where you are now. Not in my life… i'm not in your heart.

I sometimes wonder — have you moved on, or have you simply learned how to live with the silence? Are you truly happy, or just strong enough not to look back?

Will you ever love someone again the way we loved — without logic, without safety, without limits? The kind of love that stayed, even when it hurt.

Will you ever cry for someone you never met in real life — yet felt closer to than anyone you ever held?

Was the connection we felt mistaken? That quiet understanding… the way we sensed each other without words… the way distance never really felt like distance — was it all just coincidence?

Or was it something rare? In a generation full of options, endless choices, and easy exits — was choosing one person, again and again, really such an impossible thing? Does anyone else know what we shared? What we protected? What we promised?

Or does that part of you still belong only to us? I don’t know who stands beside you now. I don’t know what advice surrounds you. I don’t even know what version of me lives in your memories.

But I know this — what I felt was real. What I gave was honest. And what we had was never ordinary to me.

I don’t write this to pull you back, or to disturb your peace.

I write this because some connections don’t fade — they just become quiet. And if, in some still moment, you ever feel there is something unsaid… something unfinished… something no one else seems to understand — you already know where to find me.
